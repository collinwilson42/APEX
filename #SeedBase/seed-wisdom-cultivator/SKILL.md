## The Selection Rule: First, Do No Harm

Reject wisdom that:
- Encourages "hustle" at the expense of health
- Prioritizes "speed" at the expense of stability
- Teaches through damage rather than design

**Test:** Does this quote act as a **Guardrail** or just a **Gas Pedal**?

## Fruitful Design Principle

A quote is integrated only when it **prevents a future error**.

- ❌ Success ≠ "fixing a problem"  
- ✅ Success = "designing a reality where the problem cannot exist"

## The Vocabulary Garden

**Words are seeds. Definitions are water. Understanding is fruit.**

Every Wisdom Seed should include a **Vocabulary** section that deepens understanding of key terms. This transforms wisdom integration into language mastery — you cannot truly own an idea until you own its words.

### Vocabulary Format

For each significant word in the quote or analysis:

```markdown
## Vocabulary (Seeds → Water → Fruit)

| Word | Root/Origin | Clean Definition | Deeper Understanding |
|------|-------------|------------------|----------------------|
| [word] | [etymology] | [precise meaning] | [why this word matters here] |
```

### Selection Criteria

Include words that:
- Carry weight the reader might underestimate (e.g., "apathy" isn't just "not caring")
- Have roots that reveal hidden meaning (e.g., "courage" from *cor* = heart)
- Distinguish similar concepts (e.g., "fear" vs. "anxiety" vs. "dread")
- Appear in Stoic/philosophical texts with specific technical meaning
- Would change the reader's behavior if truly understood

### Example

> "The opposite of courage is not fear. It's apathy."

| Word | Root/Origin | Clean Definition | Deeper Understanding |
|------|-------------|------------------|----------------------|
| **Courage** | Latin *cor* (heart) | Acting rightly despite fear | Literally "with heart" — courage is not fearlessness, it's heartfulness. You feel the fear and your heart moves anyway. |
| **Apathy** | Greek *a-* (without) + *pathos* (feeling) | Absence of passion or concern | Not "calm" — *dead*. The Stoics valued *apatheia* differently: freedom from destructive passions, not absence of all feeling. Holiday's usage is the modern warning: checked-out, disengaged, spiritually asleep. |
| **Fear** | Old English *fær* (sudden danger) | Emotional response to perceived threat | Fear is *signal*, not *enemy*. It points at something worth protecting. The problem isn't feeling fear — it's letting fear choose for you. |
